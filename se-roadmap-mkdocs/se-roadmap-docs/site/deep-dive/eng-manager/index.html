    Deep Dive — Engineering Manager¶

    Prerequisite

    Engineering management requires real IC experience as its foundation. Spend 3–5 years as a senior+ engineer before making this transition.

    Career levels¶

    Focus: Transition from IC, build trust with reports, run effective 1:1s, improve team process.

    Structured 1:1 frameworks Agile / sprint facilitation Technical code review awareness Feedback models (SBI) Hiring & leveling basics Conflict resolution Roadmap basics

    Focus: Own team health and delivery, hire great engineers, manage up and across.

    Performance management cycles OKR setting Stakeholder management Technical strategy input Hiring pipelines Team health metrics Org design basics Budget awareness

    Focus: Lead multiple teams or a large team, set technical direction, partner with product leadership.

    Managing managers Cross-org program coordination Technical roadmap ownership Engineering culture Headcount & budget planning Executive communication AI tool adoption strategy

    Focus: Own engineering org, define multi-year strategy, build culture, work with board / investors.

    Org design & team topology M&A technical diligence Company-level technical vision Engineering brand & recruiting Build vs buy at org scale P&L awareness AI transformation leadership Board-level communication

    Learning path¶

    1. Be an excellent IC first (3–5 yrs · Experience) The best managers have strong technical credibility. Spend 3–5 years as a senior+ engineer.

    2. "The Manager's Path" by Camille Fournier (1 week · Book) The canonical eng management book. Read it before making the switch and re-read every year.

    3. "An Elegant Puzzle" by Will Larson (1 week · Book) Covers systems thinking for engineering orgs. Invaluable for senior managers and directors.

    4. Structured 1:1 frameworks (Ongoing · Free) Lara Hogan's resources (larahogan.me) — practical, research-backed management techniques.

    5. OKR methodology (1 week · Book) Christina Wodtke's "Radical Focus" — how to set and execute meaningful OKRs.

    6. AI tools for engineering teams (Ongoing · Experience) Learn hands-on: use Cursor, Copilot, and Claude daily. Understand what your engineers experience.

    7. Finance & headcount basics (Ongoing · Experience) Learn how P&L, headcount planning, and budget cycles work. Talk to your finance partner.

# Deep Dive — Engineering Manager

!!! warning "Prerequisite"
    Engineering management requires real IC experience as its foundation. Spend 3–5 years as a senior+ engineer before making this transition.

---

## Career levels

=== "New Manager (0–2 yrs managing)"
    **Focus:** Transition from IC, build trust with reports, run effective 1:1s, improve team process.

    `Structured 1:1 frameworks` `Agile / sprint facilitation` `Technical code review awareness` `Feedback models (SBI)` `Hiring & leveling basics` `Conflict resolution` `Roadmap basics`

=== "Manager (2–5 yrs managing)"
    **Focus:** Own team health and delivery, hire great engineers, manage up and across.

    `Performance management cycles` `OKR setting` `Stakeholder management` `Technical strategy input` `Hiring pipelines` `Team health metrics` `Org design basics` `Budget awareness`

=== "Senior Manager (5–10 yrs managing)"
    **Focus:** Lead multiple teams or a large team, set technical direction, partner with product leadership.

    `Managing managers` `Cross-org program coordination` `Technical roadmap ownership` `Engineering culture` `Headcount & budget planning` `Executive communication` `AI tool adoption strategy`

=== "Director / VP Engineering (10+ yrs managing)"
    **Focus:** Own engineering org, define multi-year strategy, build culture, work with board / investors.

    `Org design & team topology` `M&A technical diligence` `Company-level technical vision` `Engineering brand & recruiting` `Build vs buy at org scale` `P&L awareness` `AI transformation leadership` `Board-level communication`

## Learning path

1. **Be an excellent IC first** *(3–5 yrs · Experience)*
   The best managers have strong technical credibility. Spend 3–5 years as a senior+ engineer.

2. **"The Manager's Path" by Camille Fournier** *(1 week · Book)*
   The canonical eng management book. Read it before making the switch and re-read every year.

3. **"An Elegant Puzzle" by Will Larson** *(1 week · Book)*
   Covers systems thinking for engineering orgs. Invaluable for senior managers and directors.

4. **Structured 1:1 frameworks** *(Ongoing · Free)*
   Lara Hogan's resources (larahogan.me) — practical, research-backed management techniques.

5. **OKR methodology** *(1 week · Book)*
   Christina Wodtke's "Radical Focus" — how to set and execute meaningful OKRs.

6. **AI tools for engineering teams** *(Ongoing · Experience)*
   Learn hands-on: use Cursor, Copilot, and Claude daily. Understand what your engineers experience.

7. **Finance & headcount basics** *(Ongoing · Experience)*
   Learn how P&L, headcount planning, and budget cycles work. Talk to your finance partner.

## Interview topics

=== "Leadership & people"
    - Handling low performers
    - Motivating senior ICs
    - Peer conflict between reports
    - Giving difficult feedback
    - Retaining talent in competitive market

=== "Technical judgment"
    - When to take on technical debt
    - Evaluating architectural proposals
    - Balancing speed vs quality
    - Build vs buy decisions
    - AI tooling ROI assessment

=== "Process & delivery"
    - Improving team velocity
    - Reducing WIP & context switching
    - Incident post-mortem culture
    - Sprint planning at scale
    - DORA metrics interpretation

=== "Strategy"
    - Setting team OKRs
    - Roadmap prioritization
    - Stakeholder alignment techniques
    - Hiring for culture & skill
    - Growing engineers into seniors

# Engineering Manager

People, strategy, and org design. Senior engineers ready to lead teams.

!!! warning "Prerequisite"
    This is the only path that requires real IC (individual contributor) experience as its foundation. It's a transition, not an entry point. Spend 3–5 years as a senior+ engineer first.

---

## Foundation

`3–5 yrs IC experience` `Written & verbal communication` `Agile / Scrum facilitation` `CI/CD & code review awareness` `Giving & receiving feedback`

---

## Core skills

`Structured 1:1s & coaching` `Hiring & technical interviewing` `Roadmap & sprint planning` `Jira / Linear / Shortcut` `Performance reviews` `Stakeholder management`

---

## Advanced

`OKR setting & tracking` `Cross-team program coordination` `Budget & headcount planning` `Technical strategy & trade-offs` `Conflict resolution` `Executive communication`

---

## Specialization

`Org design & team topology` `M&A technical diligence` `Build vs buy decisions` `Distributed & remote leadership` `Succession planning` `Product-eng partnership`

---

!!! tip "Key insight"
    Managers who understand AI capabilities can unlock 10x productivity gains from their teams. The skill gap between AI-native and non-AI-native engineering managers is widening rapidly.
